Chapter Overview

The Wonderful World of Science introduces students to the basic foundation of scientific thinking. In this chapter, we explore how science is not just a subject in a book, but a way of looking at the universe with curiosity and logic.

Key concepts covered include:

  • Observation: The act of noticing details about the world using our five senses.
  • Inquiry: The habit of asking "Why" and "How" to find the root cause of phenomena.
  • The Scientific Method: A systematic process involving hypothesis, experimentation, and evidence-based conclusions.
  • Scientific Temper: Developing a rational and open-minded attitude towards new information.
  • Branches of Science: A brief introduction to Physics, Chemistry, and Biology.

This chapter encourages every student to realize that they are already a scientist whenever they ask a question and seek a logical answer.
